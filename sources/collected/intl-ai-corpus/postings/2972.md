Role Overview

The Senior Software Engineer, AI Enablement, plays a
hands‑on technical role in designing, developing, and
scaling AI‑powered automation solutions across internal
business units. The role focuses on embedding AI into real
operational workflows to drive measurable improvements in
productivity, quality, and efficiency.

You will partner with Principal Software Engineer,
cross‑functional teams, build rapid AI prototypes, deploy
production‑ready solutions, and create reusable assets that
accelerate adoption across global teams. Once solutions
mature, you will transition ownership and move to the next
high-impact opportunity.

This role is ideal for engineers with strong technical
depth, business problem‑solving ability, and a passion for
driving enterprise-wide AI enablement.

Key Responsibilities

1. AI Solution Design & Implementation

• Own end‑to‑end delivery of AI‑powered automation
projects—from discovery to deployment, across various
functional teams in Provation.

• Translate ambiguous business problems into concrete AI/ML
opportunities.

• Build prototypes, minimum viable solutions (MVS), and
demonstrable ROI models.

• Implement solutions using LLMs, agent frameworks, workflow
automation, REST APIs, and cloud services.

• Collaborate with cross functional teams to scale or
productionize successful pilots.

2. Cross‑Functional Collaboration

• Work directly with business stakeholders, SMEs, and
process owners to identify high‑value AI opportunities.

• Coordinate with global engineering, data, and automation
teams, including offshore centers.

3. Enablement & Knowledge Transfer

• Create reusable templates, documentation, code modules,
and training resources.

• Build playbooks and standardized modules that accelerate
repeatable rollout across business units.

4. Scaling AI Across Enterprise

• Establish a repeatable “build → enable → transition →
replicate” model for global AI adoption.

• Drive alignment with responsible AI, data governance, and
enterprise engineering standards.

• Influence internal tooling, AI platform strategy, and
long‑term architectural decisions.
