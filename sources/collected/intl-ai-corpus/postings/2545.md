testing tools, and automation frameworks for the TraceLink

Responsibilities:

The Software Development Engineer in Test assists in
building and executing automated tests for the Company's

software products. The SDET will build testing tools to
enable automated testing of the TraceLink platform

and application.

- Build and execute tests on the Tracelink platform and
  application.
- Build and deploy services for automated testing of the
  Tracelink platform persistent store.
- Execute performance tests on a Distributed Persistent Data
  store
- Create testing tools that can simulate real-world
  transaction volumes.
- Provide high-level analysis to apply appropriate
  technologies and architecture to solve conceptual problems
- Stay curious, build Innovative solution to new age technical
  issues
- Influence designs and decision with supporting data
- Work closely with engineers building the core Tracelink
  product to ensure that testability concerns are factored
  into the design of the product.
- Extend and maintain automation frameworks to enhance Test
  coverage
- Prepare and present clear reports on defect findings that
  allow developers to reproduce problems and allow the
  Quality Engineering team to verify that fixes are complete
  quickly and accurately.
- Programmatically interface testing tools with the TraceLink
  platform and application.
- Work closely with engineers building the core TraceLink
  product to ensure that testability concerns are factored
  into the design of the product.
- Research and remain informed of new technology and tools,
  and how such technologies could be applied at Tracelink.

Qualifications:

Candidates must possess the following skills and traits:

- Bachelor's degree or foreign equivalent in computer science,
  software engineering, mechanical engineering, or related
  discipline.
- 5-8 years of progressive, post-baccalaureate experience in a
  test automation engineering role involving programmatic
  interaction with a system under test.
- Experience utilizing Java, JavaScript/TypeScript, Eclipse,
  Ant or Maven, Unix, and an automated unit test framework,
