What's the Role All About?

The Software Engineer is a customer-facing, billable role
responsible for implementing, configuring, and delivering

How Will You Make an Impact?

- Partner with business stakeholders to map current workflows,
  identify ROI-backed opportunities (reduced effort,
  improved employee experience, measurable efficiency
  gains), and define future-state toAI-enabled processes.
- Design, build, and iterate AI solutions and automations in
  n8n, Copilot Studio, and other AI/automation tools; create
  reliable agents and assistants that remove manual work.
- Understand and work within NiCE internal processes and
  systems (e.g., CRMs, knowledge bases, ticketing/support
  tools, and operational platforms) to ensure solutions fit
  real-world constraints.
- Collaborate with IT and Developers when needed to overcome
  technical obstacles, integrate securely with enterprise
  systems, and scale solutions.
- Enable and upskill internal support teams to use AI
  effectively—through reusable patterns, playbooks,
  coaching, and hands-on problem solving.
- Maintain expertise in modern AI approaches and apply them
  pragmatically to internal operations, including LLMs, RAG,
  GPT-based assistants, Copilot ecosystems, MCP servers,
  APIs, JSON, and light scripting. Use AI tools to debug and
  resolve workflow or code errors.

Have You Got What It Takes?

- 2+ years of experience in solutioning, business process
  improvement, operations consulting, or similar roles
  driving cross-functional change.
- Proven ability to translate operational needs into practical
  AI use cases and deliver outcomes against clear success
  metrics.
- Strong, applied knowledge of AI and NLP technologies (LLMs,
  RAG, GPTs, Copilot-style assistants, AI agent patterns,
  MCP servers).
- Hands-on experience building automations/assistants with n8n
  (or comparable orchestration tools); comfort developing in
  Copilot Studio or similar platforms.
- Working knowledge of API integrations, JSON, and basic
  coding/scripting; able to use AI tools to debug and
  manipulate code or automation errors.
- Demonstrated curiosity and ongoing personal or professional
  use of AI tools to improve workflows—someone who
  experiments, learns fast, and brings practical AI habits
  into the role.
- Excellent stakeholder management and communication skills,
  with the ability to drive adoption and help others become
  effective with AI.
- Comfortable operating in a fast-paced environment, balancing
  multiple initiatives and iterating quickly based on
  feedback and results.
