## Description complète

About WorkOS 🚀

WorkOS builds modern developer tools and APIs that make it
easy for companies to become Enterprise Ready. Our platform
powers authentication, identity, authorization, and other
critical infrastructure that developers need to securely
scale their products to large organizations. We recently
raised a $100M Series C, valuing the company at $2B, led by
Meritech and Sapphire with participation from Greenoaks,
Craft, Abstract, and Audacious. WorkOS powers enterprise
features for many of the fastest-growing AI companies,
including OpenAI, Cursor, and Perplexity, Vercel, and Plaid.
As AI reshapes software, WorkOS is at the frontier of Human
and Agent Authentication, Identity, and Access
Control—helping companies answer a new critical question:
who are your agents, and what are they allowed to do? Our
fast-growing customer base includes hundreds of modern
software companies building the next generation of
enterprise-ready products.

About the Role

We’re building an Applied AI team to dramatically increase
productivity across Engineering, Sales, Support, and
Operations.

As an Applied AI Engineer, you’ll design and ship production
AI systems that change how WorkOS builds, sells, and
supports software. Your focus is internal: building durable
tools and workflows that teammates rely on every day.

You’ll work on a small, high‑ownership team that:

- Chooses problems based on measurable impact.
- Moves from idea → prototype → production in days or weeks.
- Adapts quickly as models, tools, and best practices evolve.

This is a 0→1 role with company‑wide visibility.

What you’ll do

- Work closely with Engineering, Sales, Support, and Ops to
  uncover high‑leverage internal problems where AI can
  remove bottlenecks or unlock new workflows.
- Design and build tools that become part of people’s daily
  work – agents, automations, and workflows that are stable,
  observable, and easy to maintain.
- Use LLMs, embeddings, retrieval, and tool‑calling to plug
  into docs, Slack, GitHub, CRM, analytics, support systems,
  and internal services.
- Replace repetitive, multi‑step manual processes with
  orchestrated, AI‑driven flows that span multiple apps and
  data sources.
- Stay current on new models and tooling, run focused
  experiments, and help the team converge on patterns,
  libraries, and infra that compound over time.

Example problems you might work on

- Turning noisy, cross‑tool workflows (tickets, Slack threads,
  docs) into a single agent that handles triage, routing,
  and suggested actions.
- Building AI‑assisted engineering workflows that shorten the
  loop from “bug report or idea in Slack” to changes safely
  deployed in production.
- Designing a GTM agent that helps reps research accounts,
  prepare meetings, and follow up using context from CRM,
  product usage, and prior conversations.

What we’re looking for

- You’ve taken AI‑powered systems from idea to production and
  through at least one iteration cycle with real users.
- Strong engineering fundamentals. You’re comfortable owning
  services, data flows, and integrations end‑to‑end. This
  role must pass our engineering interview loop.
- Experience with LLM APIs, embeddings, retrieval / RAG‑style
  architectures, and structured tool‑calling (or agents),
  even if not all in production yet.
- You’re comfortable stitching together APIs, webhooks, event
  systems, and internal tools, and you think about failure
  modes, observability, and ownership.
- Bias toward action. You care less about showing off a model
  and more about removing real bottlenecks, saving hours, or
  unlocking workflows the company couldn’t do before.
- Comfort with ambiguity and fast change. You enjoy working in
  areas where the problem, the tools, and the “right”
  patterns are evolving quickly – and you’re excited to help
  define them.

Nice to have

- Prior work with enterprise SaaS, identity / auth, or
  security‑sensitive systems.
- Exposure to MCP or similar protocols for connecting AI
  agents to real systems.
