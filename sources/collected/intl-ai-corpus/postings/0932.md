This is a Product Marketing Manager role focused on go-to-
market strategy, positioning, and marketing content for
Amplitude's AI analytics platform. While the role requires
technical fluency with AI product development workflows and

tuning, or deploying AI models directly. The role exists to
market and position AI/analytics products that other teams
build.

## Description complète

Amplitude is the leading AI analytics platform, helping over
4,300 customers—including Atlassian, Burger King,
NBCUniversal, Square, and Under Armour—build better products
and digital experiences. With powerful AI Agents embedded
across our platform, teams can analyze, test, and optimize
user experiences faster than ever. Ranked #1 across multiple
categories by G2, Amplitude is the best-in-class solution
for product, data, and marketing teams. Learn more at
amplitude.com.

As an organization, we deliver for our customers by living
our values. We operate from a place of humility, take
ownership of problems and successes, approach challenges
with a growth mindset, and put our customers at the center
of everything we do.

Amplitude’s Commitment to Diversity Equity & Inclusion
(DEI): Amplitude believes that diversity enables the
creation of better products, improves the ability to solve
complex problems, and drives more powerful solutions. We
strive to create an environment of inclusion—one focused on
psychological safety, empathy, and human connection—that
will allow employees of all backgrounds to thrive.

Product development is fundamentally being transformed by
AI. As coding gets dramatically faster, the critical
bottlenecks shift: What should we build? Which AI model
drives the outcomes we care about? Did this feature actually
drive the outcomes we care about?  This shift is creating a
new software development lifecycle in which analytics plays
a mission critical role where Product Managers have the
autonomy to test hypotheses, measure AI model performance,
and iterate based on real outcomes.

Amplitude is building the platform that enables this new
reality— Our experimentation and analytics stack gives PMs
the tools to work more effectively with engineering: faster
feedback loops, clearer impact measurement, and the
confidence to make bold product bets.

About the role

As a Sr. Product Marketing Manager, you will: partner with
our product, GTM, and customer teams to position Amplitude
as the platform that democratizes data-driven product
development—empowering PMs to act with speed and conviction
in the AI era.

We're looking for a Sr. Product Marketing Manager to
position Amplitude as the platform that empowers product
builders to ship, test, and optimize AI products with
confidence. You'll own go-to-market strategy for our Feature
Experimentation, Feature Flags, SDKs and LLM Analytics—the
stack that enables Product teams to move faster, experiment
fearlessly, and make decisions based on real user outcomes,
not gut instinct.

This is a high-impact role for someone who understands both
product development workflows and has the technical fluency
to bridge Product and Engineering teams. You'll help PMs
gain the autonomy to test ideas, validate hypotheses, and
drive product velocity without constant engineering
dependencies.

What you will own

Shape the narrative for product development

- Articulate why analytics becomes exponentially more critical
  as AI accelerates product development—when you can build
  10x faster, knowing what to build and whether it worked
  becomes the competitive edge
- Position Amplitude as the platform that gives Product teams
  the autonomy to test AI models, validate features, and
  drive outcomes without engineering bottlenecks
- Tell the story of how experimentation culture transforms
  product velocity—from "let's discuss what might work" to
  "let's test and know what works"

Bridge Product and Engineering with authentic fluency

- Develop messaging that resonates with Product Managers while
  demonstrating enough technical depth to earn credibility
  with Engineering partners
- Create content that helps PMs understand how to leverage
  experimentation, feature flags, and analytics throughout
  the product lifecycle—not just at launch
- Speak comfortably about product development workflows:
  hypothesis testing, gradual rollouts, AI model evaluation,
  conversion optimization, and impact measurement

Drive adoption across product organizations

- Lead launches for experimentation, feature management, and
  LLM analytics capabilities with a focus on PM workflows
  and time-to-value
- Build enablement programs that help sales and customer
  success teams speak to product organizations about
  experimentation culture, analytics maturity, and AI-
  readiness
- Partner with product to shape roadmap based on how modern
  product teams want to work—faster feedback loops, clearer
  impact visibility, more autonomy

Build compelling product stories with customer evidence

- Work with customers building AI products to showcase how
  experimentation enables product velocity at scale
- Create case studies that demonstrate real outcomes:
  increases in user satisfaction, lifts in conversion,
  ability to safely test models on small cohorts
- Translate "we measure real things instead of fake things"
  into repeatable playbooks for product teams across
  different stages and verticals

Position Amplitude's unique value for product teams

- Differentiate Amplitude from developer-first tools by
  emphasizing how we empower Product teams with analytics
  autonomy, not just engineers
- Identify gaps where product teams struggle to get fast,
  actionable insights from their experimentation and
  analytics tools
- Articulate why analytics is becoming more load-bearing as AI
  makes building faster but deciding what to build harder

What we are looking for:

- 6+ years of product marketing experience in B2B SaaS,
  ideally with exposure to product development tools,
  experimentation platforms, or analytics products
- Product development fluency: You understand modern product
  workflows—how teams prioritize, ship, measure, and
  iterate. You can speak credibly about experimentation
  methodologies, hypothesis testing, and data-driven
  decision making
- Ability to bridge Product and Engineering: You're technical
  enough to understand how things work under the hood and
  can translate between PM priorities and engineering
  constraints. You don't need to write code, but you
  understand the product development lifecycle
- Experience with experimentation or analytics tools: Hands-on
  understanding of A/B testing, feature flags, or product
  analytics—ideally through direct use or through marketing
  these capabilities
- Exceptional storytelling: Ability to craft narratives that
  resonate with Product leaders—connecting product velocity,
  experimentation culture, and business outcomes in
  compelling ways
- Cross-functional leadership: Proven ability to partner
  effectively with product, sales, customer success, and
  marketing teams to drive launches and adoption

Preferred Qualifications:

- Hands-on experience with digital analytics and Amplitude a
  plus
- Savvy with AI tools and a desire to readily leverage these
  tools to scale.
- Familiarity with analytics tools such as Amplitude or
  similar platforms.
- Solid technical chops and an ability to quickly dive in and
  learn new technologies.
- Hands-on experience with customers and full-funnel
  marketing.

What makes you successful in this role:

- Product empathy: You understand what PMs care about
  (velocity, autonomy, impact visibility, confidence in
  decisions) and can authentically speak to those priorities
- Technical curiosity: You ask good questions, learn quickly,
  and can explain technical concepts to non-technical
  audiences without oversimplifying
- Comfort with ambiguity: AI-native product development is
  still emerging. You're energized by shaping narratives in
  new categories rather than executing established playbooks
- Bias toward action: You ship, iterate, and leverage AI tools
  to move faster. You test hypotheses with real customers
  and refine based on feedback
- Experimentation mindset: You practice what you preach—using
  data to inform decisions, running tests when you're
  uncertain, and measuring what matters
