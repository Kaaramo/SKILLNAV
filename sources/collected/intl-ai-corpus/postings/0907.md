## Description complète

Let’s fix hospitality, for good.

Hospitality is tough – margins are thin, waste is high, and
teams are stretched. But it doesn’t have to be this hard.
That’s why we built Nory.

Our CEO, Conor, knows the pain first-hand. After founding
and scaling Mad Egg in Ireland, he got fed up with juggling
“market-leading” systems, clunky spreadsheets, and endless
printouts. So he set out to build the tool he wished he’d
had from day one.

Nory is an all-knowing restaurant management system. It
blends real-time data with AI predictive analytics, giving
operators control of their margins. From food prep to
forecasting, it’s operational intelligence that helps
restaurants run with consistency, certainty, and profit. The
result? Thriving restaurants, better jobs, less waste,
healthier margins. Learn more about what we are building
here.

And we’re just getting started. Fresh off a Series B led by
Kinnevik, we’ve grown to 80+ people across Ireland, the UK,
Spain and New York – and demand is scaling faster than we
ever imagined.

The role

We are now hiring our first Senior AI Product Engineer -
first and foremost, a senior software engineer who can
architect, build, operate, and own production-grade backend
systems that use agentic workflows and LLMs. This is not a
research role and not a chatbot feature role. You will be
building reliable, scalable backend services that run in
production and deliver real-time intelligence to thousands
of operators. If you are a senior engineer who has built,
shipped, and run multi-step agentic systems in production,
you will thrive here.

What you’ll be doing

As a Senior AI Product Engineer, you will own AI-powered
backend systems end-to-end, from design through to live
operation.

- Owning production AI systems, including reliability,
  monitoring, alerting, and on-call responsibility
- Designing and building backend services that incorporate
  multi-step agentic workflows
- Turning prototypes and ideas into shipped, customer-facing
  features, then iterating based on real usage
- Designing agent workflows with explicit handling of
  failures, retries, and edge cases
- Integrating LLM-powered capabilities into core Nory
  workflows in collaboration with Product and Data
- Making pragmatic trade-offs around latency, cost, accuracy,
  and user trust
- Establishing best practices for how Nory builds and operates
  agentic systems at scale

Your first 6 months at Nory

- Take ownership of existing agent prototypes and ship them as
  stable, production-ready systems
- Deliver at least one agent-backed feature used by real
  restaurant operators
- Design and implement monitoring, evaluation, and failure
  handling for live agent systems
- Partner closely with Product and Data to ensure AI features
  drive clear user and commercial outcomes
- Establish patterns and practices that make AI systems easier
  to build, operate, and scale across teams

What you’ll bring

This role is designed for engineers who have personally
built, shipped, and operated production backend systems that
use LLMs or agentic workflows.

- Strong backend engineering fundamentals. You can design APIs
  and services, write maintainable code, and reason about
  system architecture, scalability, and SLAs. We use Python,
  but language matters less than engineering depth.
- Production experience with agentic workflows. You have owned
  multi-step agent systems in production, including tool
  use, orchestration, retries, and failure handling.
  Experience limited to demos, chatbots, or internal
  experiments is not sufficient for this role.
- Ownership of long-lived systems. You are comfortable
  monitoring, debugging, and iterating on live services over
  time.
- Applied LLM experience in real products. You’ve worked with
  prompts, embeddings, retrieval, and evaluation in
  production systems – pragmatically, not experimentally.
- High autonomy in ambiguous environments. You are comfortable
  owning problems end-to-end, making trade-offs, and moving
  forward without perfect specs.
- Product and commercial mindset. You make technical decisions
  based on user behaviour, operational impact, and business
  outcomes.

Nice-to-have:

- Background in operational, B2B, or data-heavy products
- Experience operating AI systems at scale or in high-traffic
  environments
- Experience helping define internal engineering standards or
  best practices
- Familiarity with cloud infrastructure, data platforms, or
  analytics pipelines
