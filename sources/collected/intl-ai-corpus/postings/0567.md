## Description complète

Principal Operations Engineer (Automation & AI)

Location: UK or Portugal

Department: Engineering

Employment Type: Contract (B2B) (with potential to
transition to permanent)

Contract Length: 6 months (with potential for extension)

About YLD:

Our goal is to help our clients keep moving forward. We work
with them to build the skills and capabilities they need to
stay ahead of the competition, and we know our success only
happens when they succeed. We’re a team of curious, talented
people, and we’re committed to making YLD a place where
great people want to work, grow, and stay.

We’re a remote-first consultancy specialising in software
engineering, product design, and data. Our teams are based
across London, Lisbon, and Porto, and we create digital
solutions that continue to deliver value long after our work
is done.

About the role:

As a Principal Operations Engineer at YLD, you will help
remove friction from how YLD operates.

This role will start as a senior consulting engagement. The
intent is to move quickly, deliver tangible improvements,
and learn what the long-term shape of this capability should
be. Based on outcomes and mutual fit, there may be an
opportunity to transition into a permanent role, but this is
not assumed upfront.

This is a senior, hands-on role for someone who can move
comfortably between business problems and technical
solutions. You will work across People, Finance,
Engineering, Product Design, Client Services, and
Commercial, identifying where work is slow, manual, or
overly complex and fixing it.

This is not a coordination or advisory role. You will be
expected to design, build, automate, and ship improvements
yourself, using code, automation, and AI as first-class
tools.

The role reports to the COO and works closely with the
leadership team. Regular travel to Portugal is expected to
stay close to teams and how work actually happens.

 Your day-to-day responsibilities will look like
this:Eliminate operational friction

- Identify high-leverage operational pain points across the
  business
- Remove unnecessary steps, manual work, and duplicated effort
- Replace fragile processes with simple, resilient systems
- Focus on outcomes, not documentation for its own sake

Build and automate internal systems

- Design and build internal tools, workflows, and automations
- Integrate and extend existing platforms (HRIS, finance
  systems, CRM, access management, internal tooling),
  working closely with external partners where needed to
  ensure systems fit together cleanly and workflows work
  end-to-end
- Use scripting, APIs, low-code, and AI where they
  meaningfully improve speed and consistency
- Treat internal operations as a product, with users, feedback
  loops, and iteration

Apply AI pragmatically

- Identify where AI can remove cognitive or manual load
- Implement AI-assisted workflows that teams actually adopt
- Balance speed with safety, judgement, and data
  responsibility
- Focus on practical leverage rather than experimentation
  theatre

Work closely with teams and leadership

- Work directly with Client Partners, People, Finance, Heads
  of, and the leadership team
- Understand how work happens in practice and where
  constraints show up
- Deliver improvements in small, practical increments
- Reduce reliance on ad-hoc escalation and manual coordination

Create sustainable foundations

- Ensure improvements are understandable, maintainable, and
  clearly owned
- Document systems and processes where it materially reduces
  risk, improves continuity, or supports scale
- Help define what longer-term operational capability should
  look like as YLD continues to scale

You’ll have the following skills and experience:

- Strong engineering background (software, platform,
  automation, or systems engineering)
- Proven experience applying technical solutions to business
  or operational problems
- Comfort working across non-technical teams such as People,
  Finance, and Commercial
- High judgement and the confidence to make trade-offs
- Experience using automation and AI in real workflows, not
  just demos

This role is for you if:

- You are an engineer who enjoys solving business and
  operational problems
- You like working end-to-end, from messy problem to shipped
  solution
- You are comfortable operating with ambiguity and imperfect
  information
- You focus on simplifying how work gets done by removing
  unnecessary steps and manual effort.
- You think in systems but stay close to how people actually
  work
- You see AI and automation as leverage, not magic
- You get satisfaction from making other people’s work easier
  and faster

This role is not for you if:

- You want a strategy or advisory-only role
- You prefer writing recommendations over building solutions
- You are uncomfortable touching real systems or tooling
- You expect fully defined requirements before starting
- You want to manage a large team rather than ship yourself
- You are most comfortable working in heavily governed,
  committee-driven environments

What success looks like in the first months:

- High-friction operational workflows have been simplified or
  removed
- Manual work has been replaced with automation where it makes
  sense
- Teams report clearer ownership and less operational drag
- Leadership time spent on day-to-day operational problem-
  solving is visibly reduced
- Internal systems feel easier, faster, and more predictable
  to use, with clear ownership and lightweight documentation
  where it matters
