## Description complète

Who We Are Looking For

We are looking for  a technical leader and subject matter
expert in Agentic AI, responsible for defining the
architectural goals and implementation strategies for our
AI-driven workflows. When designing agentic systems, this
leader performs deep dives into clinical business needs—such
as ambient listening, scheduling, and documentation—before
selecting the appropriate LLM orchestration tools and
strategies. This leader would work closely with product
managers and data scientists to translate complex business
problems into scalable, agentic solutions that enhance
clinical efficiency and the patient experience.

What You’ll Be Doing As A Part of Our Team

Architectural Strategy & Agentic Design

- Problem Decomposition: Excel at decomposing complex clinical
  workflows into discrete, reasoning-based AI agents,
  organizing the architectural strategies needed to
  implement them.
- Agentic Orchestration: Define the root business problems and
  use that knowledge to design integrated AI solutions
  (e.g., ambient listening for documentation or automated
  waitlist management) across multiple applications.
- Reference Architecture Alignment: Work with the company’s
  architecture team to ensure AI agents are built on shared
  services and systems, reducing waste and maintaining a
  cohesive software ecosystem.
- Innovation & Competitiveness: Set goals around emerging AI
  standards and agentic frameworks to keep the business
  competitive in the rapidly evolving AI-native EMR market.

Execution & Partnership

- Collaborative Leadership: Partner with internal Data
  Scientists and third-party AI engineers to drive project
  execution, serving as the technical anchor for the team
  during the staff augmentation phase and beyond.
- Agile AI Development: Apply agile and lean principles to AI
  development, timeboxing experiments and focusing on
  delivering immediate clinical value through iterative
  agent refinement.
- Cross-Functional Alignment: Work with stakeholders outside
  of engineering to align AI capabilities with Product and
  UX goals, ensuring agents are streamlined and intuitive
  for healthcare providers.
- Documentation & Evangelism: Maintain strong documentation
  practices to evangelize new AI development patterns and
  gain adoption of agentic practices across the department.

What You Should Have to Qualify

- Education & Experience: Bachelor’s degree in Computer
  Science, AI/ML, or a related technical field, with 10+
  years of professional engineering experience, including
  significant time spent in a Principal Engineer or Lead
  Architect capacity.
- Agentic AI Architecture: Deep technical proficiency in
  designing multi-agent orchestration systems. You must have
  hands-on experience with frameworks (e.g., LangGraph,
  AutoGen, CrewAI) and a sophisticated understanding of
  agentic patterns like planning, tool-use, and self-
  reflection.
- Hybrid Team Leadership: Proven ability to provide technical
  "anchor" leadership in a hybrid environment, effectively
  directing the work of third-party staff augmentation while
  maintaining absolute ownership of the internal quality and
  architectural standards.
- System Decomposition & Modeling: Expert-level ability to
  take a messy clinical workflow (like a PT initial
  evaluation) and decompose it into a structured data model
  and a sequence of AI-driven reasoning steps.
- Strategic Problem Solving: A track record of "Deep Dives"
  into complex business domains, translating nebulous
  clinical needs into precise technical requirements that
  align with a broader Reference Architecture.
- Engineering Rigor in AI: Experience moving AI from
  "prototype/notebook" to "production-grade," including
  implementing evaluation frameworks (LLM-as-a-judge),
  monitoring for agent drift, and ensuring deterministic
  outcomes in non-deterministic systems.

Ideally, You Would Also Have These

- Healthcare Domain Knowledge: Familiarity with clinical
  documentation standards, HIPAA compliance, and the nuances
  of EMR data sets.
- Vector Database Experience: High-level understanding of
  retrieval strategies (RAG) to support agentic reasoning
  and long-term memory.
- Team Transition Experience: Previous experience leading a
  hybrid team of internal and external resources, with the
  ability to take full ownership of the technology stack
  post-vendor disengagement.
