### Raisonnement classification (LLM upstream)

This role is on an AI Enablement team building a platform
for OTHER teams to ship AI-powered features. The engineer
works ON the platform infrastructure (backend MCP services,
frontend widget host, contribution models) rather than
directly building, training, fine-tuning, or deploying AI/ML
models. The LLM integration work involves shaping platform
boundaries and tool invocation flows—enabling
infrastructure, not hands-on model work.

Job Description

Job description



The AI Enablement team is building a unified platform that
empowers engineering teams to rapidly and safely ship
AI‑powered features. As a Tech Lead / Senior Full-Stack
Engineer, you will provide architectural leadership across
backend (MCP infrastructure, services) and frontend (widget
host app, UI contribution surface). You will define and
evolve the contribution models that enable teams to plug
functionality into the platform consistently.

This role requires strong technical breadth across .NET and
Node/React, a strict test-first mindset, the ability to
define and defend architectural boundaries, and a platform-
team mentality where success is measured by what other teams
deliver using your foundation.

Key Responsibilities

Architecture & Full-Stack Leadership

- Lead architectural decisions across backend APIs, MCP
  services, frontend contributions, and cross-cutting
  platform components.
- Define and evolve contribution models and extension
  frameworks across UI and backend surfaces.
- Ensure clear boundaries between platform, product, and
  extension layers—and be able to defend them technically
  and organizationally.
- Guide teams in system design, scalability, observability,
  and maintainability.

Hands-On Development

- Contribute production-grade code across both .NET backend
  services and Node/React/TypeScript frontend components.
- Build APIs, platform capabilities, UI frameworks, and
  developer tooling aligned with platform simplicity and
  extensibility.
- Maintain high performance, security, reliability, and DX-
  first design.

Test-First Engineering

- Champion a strict test-first approach: TDD, contract
  testing, integration tests, automated quality gates.
- Build testing standards that apply consistently across
  backend and frontend surfaces.

LLM Platform Integration

- Apply a pragmatic understanding of LLM fundamentals—tokens,
  context windows, tool descriptions—to shape platform
  boundaries.
- Collaborate with the AI platform to design tool invocation
  flows, prompt interfaces, and safe execution patterns.
- Deep theoretical AI knowledge is not required—but rapid,
  confident learning and hands-on integration is expected.

Platform Team Mindset

- Operate with a service mindset: your success is defined by
  what other teams build successfully on your platform.
- Create documentation, reference implementations,
  architectural guardrails, SDK patterns, and contribution
  guidelines.
- Drive alignment across engineering groups to ensure the
  platform becomes the default mechanism for AI integration.





Qualifications

Qualifications

Required Qualifications

- Strong expertise in both:
  - .NET (C#) for backend and service development
  - Node.js, React, and TypeScript for frontend and platform UI
    engineering
- Proven experience designing and implementing large-scale
  architectures across the stack.
- Strong ability to define and maintain clear architectural
  boundaries, governance, and ownership.
- Deep experience with test-first development (TDD, contract
  tests, UI tests, CI/CD quality gates).
- Practical experience integrating or building with LLM-based
  platforms (tokens, context windows, tool schemas).
- Strong communicator who collaborates across teams and
  influences engineering direction.

Preferred Qualifications

- Experience building internal developer platforms,
  extensibility frameworks, contribution surfaces, plug-in
  architectures, or micro-frontends.
- Experience with MCP, tool architectures, or agent
  frameworks.
- Exposure to cloud-native platforms, distributed systems, and
  high-scale production environments.
- Understanding of platform governance—versioning, schema
  contracts, compatibility layers, security boundaries.

What Success Looks Like

- A clear, scalable architecture that accelerates—rather than
  restricts—teams integrating AI.
- Multiple teams ship AI features quickly using your
  contribution models and platform components.
- Back-end and front-end surfaces remain clean, governed, and
  easy to extend.
- The platform remains stable, testable, and well-documented.
- You become a trusted architectural voice across teams and
  leadership.
