Job Description

Job Posting Title

Principal Engineer – Data & AI

Job Description

Consulting Services, a part of GE Vernova, has offices in
multiple countries and offers our global clients (external &
internal GE) a wide range of solutions across the entire
spectrum of power generation, delivery, and utilization. Our
Power Systems Engineering team utilizes their deep knowledge
of connected power grid planning, design, operation, and
life-cycle management, to offer customized solutions to our
global clients. Our Power Economics team offers analyses
focused on the understanding and the study of the financial
and physical operation of the electric power systems
including generation and grid planning, system optimization,
asset valuation, competitive power markets and energy policy
implications. Our software business plays a critical role in
delivering these solutions by developing and maintaining
cutting-edge analytics platforms for production cost
modeling, capacity expansion, reliability assessment, market
simulation and both steady-state and transient stability
simulation. These tools are continuously enhanced based on
feedback from consulting engagements and evolving industry
needs. Over 700 entities worldwide use our suite of software
tools to assess the economics, performance, and reliability
of interconnected power systems. We also offer a full range
of education courses through our Power Systems Engineering
Course.

GE Vernova’s Consulting Services business is advancing the
future of power system planning, simulation, and decision
support for utilities, system operators, and regulators
worldwide. We are seeking a Principal Engineer – Data & AI
to provide technical leadership across the data, reporting,
and AI foundations of our planning platform PlanOS.

In this role, you will own the end-to-end data strategy
supporting long-term transmission and generation planning,
capacity expansion, reliability assessment, and scenario
analysis. You will drive the design of robust, auditable,
and reproducible data models, ensure scalable execution of
large multi-scenario planning studies, and lead the
responsible adoption of AI to enhance insight,
explainability, and decision-making—while maintaining the
rigor required in regulated environments.

You will operate at the intersection of software
engineering, power system planning, data architecture, and
applied AI, partnering closely with product teams, domain
experts, and customer-facing organizations.

Roles & Responsibilities

- Own the end-to-end data architecture and reporting strategy
  for PlanOS planning platform.
- Design and govern data models and schemas for planning
  inputs, intermediate results, and outputs.
- Ensure data versioning, lineage, and reproducibility to
  support auditable and defensible planning results.
- Optimize storage and data access patterns for large, multi-
  scenario and multi-year planning studies.
- Enable clear, consistent reporting by translating complex
  planning results into dashboards, narratives, and
  interactive analysis tools for planners, executives, and
  regulators.
- Identify and lead AI/ML use cases that add value to long-
  term grid planning.
- Ensure AI methods are explainable, traceable, and
  appropriate for regulated planning environments.
- Partner with engineering teams to integrate data and AI
  workflows into production-grade planning software.
- Act as a technical bridge between software teams, power
  system experts, and customer-facing organizations.
- Own data governance, quality assurance, and validation
  processes across the planning platform.
- Balance innovation with rigor when introducing new data and
  AI capabilities into critical planning workflows.

Required Qualifications

- Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Computer Science,
  Electrical Engineering, Power Systems, Data Engineering,
  or a related discipline.
- 10+ years of experience in data engineering, data
  architecture, platform engineering, or related roles.
- Strong hands-on experience with Python and SQL in enterprise
  software systems.
- Proven expertise in designing and maintaining robust data
  models and schemas for complex platforms.
- Experience with large-scale analytical, simulation, or
  optimization-driven systems.
- Demonstrated ability to provide technical leadership across
  teams without direct authority.
- Strong written and verbal communication skills, with the
  ability to explain complex technical concepts to diverse
  audiences.

Desired Characteristics

- Experience with grid planning, power system simulation, or
  energy market tools (e.g., PSLF, PlanOS, or similar
  platforms).
- Familiarity with optimization and planning frameworks such
  as Pyomo, CVXPY, OR-Tools, Gurobi, or CPLEX.
- Experience designing enterprise-grade APIs and persistence
  layers, including ORM-based data access (e.g., SQLAlchemy)
  and long-lived service frameworks (e.g., Pyramid).
- Hands-on experience with cloud-based data platforms (AWS or
  Azure), including data lakes and distributed compute.
- Background in data versioning, lineage, and auditability for
  regulated or high-assurance environments.
- Practical experience introducing AI/ML into production
  systems with a focus on explainability and governance.
- Strong systems-thinking mindset with the ability to balance
  near-term delivery with long-term platform evolution.
- Passion for building trusted, high-impact software that
  supports critical infrastructure planning.

Business Acumen

- Demonstrates the initiative to explore alternate technology
  and approaches to solving problems
- Skilled in breaking down problems, documenting problem
  statements and estimating efforts
- Demonstrates awareness about competitors and industry
  trends.
- Has the ability to analyze impact of technology choices.
