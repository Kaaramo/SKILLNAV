## Description complète

What we do

Saccharo AI builds human‑centric, agentic workflows that
help organizations capture more large-scale sales
opportunities through RFPs (Requests for Proposals). We’ve
closed our angel round, are proving product‑market fit with
pilot customers, and are scaling toward general release.

The RFP‑software market has already topped $1.5 billion and
is on track to more than double this decade, underscoring a
once‑in‑a‑generation chance to modernize how proposals are
won. We plan to become the partner of choice for teams ready
to ride this inflection point.

Saccharo’s platform ingests unstructured RFP documents,
surfaces the must‑win opportunities, and orchestrates
AI‑driven workflows—retrieval, qualification, and strategy
generation—that let proposal managers focus on high‑value
thinking instead of copy‑paste drudgery.

We’re looking for an AI‑savvy engineer to help us extend
these custom data pipelines, refine our retrieval systems,
and train collaborative agentic models that keep our
customers a step ahead.

To learn more, visit our site.

What you’ll do

- Build & maintain data pipelines for ingestion, cleaning, and
  embedding of unstructured proposal content
- Prototype, fine‑tune, and evaluate open‑weight LLMs;
  implement retrieval‑augmented generation (RAG) for
  grounded answers
- Deploy models behind scalable APIs and monitor latency,
  cost, and drift
- Embed responsible‑AI controls (e.g., privacy, transparency,
  bias checks)
- Collaborate cross‑functionally with product, sales, and
  customer SMEs to translate user pain points into
  ML‑powered features
- Mentor team members on best practices; contribute to
  engineering playbooks and code review rituals

Who you are

- 3 + years of Python in production (Pandas, FastAPI,
  PyTorch/TensorFlow)
- Hands‑on experience with cloud infra (Docker, CI/C) and
  vector databases (Pinecone)
- Track record shipping ML models from experiment to prod; LLM
  or NLP domain preferred
- Strong written communication; able to document experiments
  and draft lightweight RFCs
- Bias toward action, low ego, and eagerness to work
  shoulder‑to‑shoulder with the CTO
