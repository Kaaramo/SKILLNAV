## Description complète

About Us

At SentinelOne, we’re redefining cybersecurity by pushing
the limits of what’s possible—leveraging AI-powered, data-
driven innovation to stay ahead of tomorrow’s threats.

From building industry-leading products to cultivating an
exceptional company culture, our core values guide
everything we do. We’re looking for passionate individuals
who thrive in collaborative environments and are eager to
drive impact. If you’re excited about solving complex
challenges in bold, innovative ways, we’d love to connect
with you.

What are we looking for?

We’re looking for a data scientist who’s deeply motivated to
build high-quality datasets that power cybersecurity-focused
AI. Your primary mission will be to design and maintain the
data foundations behind products that detect threats,
summarize alerts, and enable defenders. You should be
energized by ambiguity, eager to explore noisy and complex
security data, and experienced in transforming it into
structured, useful assets.

While your main focus will be on data—collecting, cleaning,
labeling, and maintaining—you’ll also help define how we
measure quality and track progress over time. We’re
especially excited about candidates who understand the
nuances of cybersecurity and want to shape how AI is applied
to real-world threats.

What will you do?

- Build and maintain datasets that power training, fine-
  tuning, and prompting of AI models for cybersecurity
  applications.
- Explore and curate structured and unstructured security data
  (logs, malware, alerts, incidents) to improve model
  relevance and effectiveness.
- Develop pipelines for data collection, cleaning, labeling,
  and versioning.
- Collaborate with researchers and engineers to define data
  requirements and ensure relevance to cybersecurity
  problems.
- Contribute to evaluation design and automation to ensure
  continuous improvement of AI systems.
- Drive data quality and coverage for use cases like
  summarization, classification, detection, and reasoning
  over security context.

What skills and experience should you bring?

- 5+ years of experience working with data in applied AI or ML
  systems, ideally in security or similarly complex domains.
- Strong Python skills and experience with common data tools
  (pandas, numpy, PySpark).
- Experience curating and maintaining large, evolving datasets
  for training and evaluation.
- Familiarity with data labeling strategies, data versioning,
  and pipeline automation.
- Ability to explore and analyze messy data from real-world
  systems.
- Experience with LLMs or generative AI models, and
  understanding of their data dependencies.
- A working knowledge of cybersecurity concepts, threat types,
  telemetry sources, or detection workflows.

Bonus points for:

- Hands-on experience with cybersecurity data (EDR, SIEM,
  IDS/IPS, threat intel, malware, etc.).
- Familiarity with open-source eval tools (HELM, lm-eval, BIG-
  bench, etc.).
- Experience with MLOps stacks (Airflow, MLFlow, Weights &
  Biases).
- Infrastructure skills: cloud platforms, Kubernetes, data
  pipelines, IaC.
- A passion for building tools that make security
  professionals more effective.
