## Description complète

Senior Data Engineer – Search and AI Platform

About the role:

The Search and AI Platform supports core products such as
Scopus, ScienceDirect, and AI‑driven research solutions. As
a Senior Data Engineer, you will help design, build, and run
reliable data pipelines and services that support
large‑scale search and analytics use cases. You will work
hands‑on while also supporting and guiding other engineers.

About the team:

The Search and AI Platform is Elsevier’s agentic data
platform, which powers products such as Scopus,
ScienceDirect, and our next-generation LLM-powered research
systems. The platform utilises agentic services to
interrogate our rich knowledge graphs, search and
recommendation systems, and our unparalleled collection of
research data, delivering insights to the scientific
community that enable them to collaborate more effectively,
work smarter, and produce high-quality research more
efficiently.

Responsibilities:

- Design, build, and maintain scalable data pipelines and
  services
- Develop batch and ELT/ETL data workflows for ingesting and
  processing large datasets
- Work with technologies such as Python, Java, Spark, SQL, and
  Airflow
- Apply good software engineering practices, including
  testing, documentation, and CI/CD
- Contribute to cloud‑based systems (AWS), focusing on
  reliability, monitoring, and automation
- Collaborate with product, platform, and data science
  partners to deliver meaningful outcomes
- Support team learning through code reviews and knowledge
  sharing

Requirements:

- Experience working as a data or software engineer on
  production systems
- Strong skills in Python and experience with at least one
  backend language (such as Java)
- Understanding of data engineering concepts such as data
  lakes, batch processing, and distributed systems
- Experience building or operating data pipelines using tools
  like Spark and Airflow
- Familiarity with cloud platforms (AWS preferred) and
  production monitoring
- Clear communication skills and a collaborative approach to
  problem‑solving
